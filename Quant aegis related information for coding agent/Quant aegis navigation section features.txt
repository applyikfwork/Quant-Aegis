13. Continuous Learning Pipeline

After every completed trade, the system:

1. Records the original market conditions.


2. Compares the prediction with the actual outcome.


3. Measures forecast accuracy.


4. Identifies which agents were most accurate.


5. Updates agent performance statistics.


6. Adjusts future confidence weighting based on long-term evidence.


7. Stores the completed case for future evaluation and strategy refinement.



This is an evidence-based feedback system rather than automatic model retraining, reducing the risk of degrading performance from short-term market noise.

The AI can find opportunities, strategies can generate trades, and users can execute positions — but Risk Center decides:

Is this safe?

How much capital can be used?

What is the maximum acceptable loss?

Should the trade be reduced?

Should the system block execution?


It is the final protection layer.

A professional trading system does not try to avoid all losses.

It controls:

Loss size

Exposure

Probability

Survival

Long-term consistency


Risk Center protects the account from:

Bad strategies

Emotional decisions

Excessive leverage

Market crashes

AI mistakes

Correlated positions
